## HOOK 1: The Problem Nobody's Talking About

**Current Reality:**
- Companies hire teams of expensive engineers: $200K-$500K per person per year
- A single complex project takes 6-12 months
- 40% of software projects fail or go wildly over budget
- We're stuck in a 1960s model of software development (people writing code one line at a time)

**The Hidden Cost:**
But worse than the money... it's the *lost potential*.

How many brilliant ideas never got built because "it would take too long"?
How many companies didn't pivot because "we can't afford to rewrite that"?
How many students have a vision but can't afford the team to build it?

---

## HOOK 2: What If AI Could Actually Collaborate?

Right now, when you use ChatGPT or Claude:
- You get one perspective
- You get one approach
- You get one answer

**But humans don't work that way.**

When a great team solves a hard problem, something magical happens:
- One person proposes an idea
- Another challenges it
- A third sees a pattern others missed
- They iterate, synthesize, challenge assumptions
- Something *novel* emerges that none of them could have created alone

**Nobody's built that for AI yet.**

**That's not fantasy.** That's what we've already proven in Week 1.
